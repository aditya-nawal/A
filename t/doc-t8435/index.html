DJI Flysafe is a technology installed in DJI drones that helps drone pilots fly safely and responsibly. It includes things like geofencing, which prevents drones from flying in certain areas, and a flight data recorder, which records information about the drone’s flight path in case of an accident. First, let us take a look at DJI Geofenced Areas and understand it clearly. Understanding DJI Geofenced Areas DJI’s Geo-fenced map | Source: DJI Geo-fencing is a software feature that restricts drone flights in certain areas. Let’s try to understand the various geofenced areas. Start by identifying whether your desired flight area is within a geofenced zone using DJI’s Fly Safe Geo Map. Here’s how: Access the Geo Map: Visit DJI’s Fly Safe Geo Map website. Enter Location: Type in your flight location’s address in the search box. Check Zones: Ensure “Warning Zones” and “Enhanced Warning Zones” are selected for a complete geofencing overview. Color Codes In DJI Geofencing Zones Geofencing zones are color-coded for easy identification: Red Zones: Restricted areas needing a Custom Unlock. Gray Zones: Areas with altitude limits, typically near airports, not subject to unlocking. Blue Zones: Locations where flying is risky but permitted with a Self Unlock. Yellow Zones: Potentially dangerous areas, alerting pilots without requiring unlocking. What is DJI Flysafe Unlocking?
