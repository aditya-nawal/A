While the book has received a lot of praise for its insightful and practical wisdom, it has also sparked controversy due to some of its controversial aspects. One of the most controversial aspects of "12 Rules for Life" is Peterson's stance on gender and identity politics. Peterson is known for his opposition to Bill C-16, a Canadian legislation that added gender identity and expression as protected grounds to the Canadian Human Rights Act. He argues that the legislation would compel speech and that it is a violation of free speech rights. This stance has earned him criticism from those who support the legislation and advocate for transgender rights. Additionally, Peterson has been criticized for his views on traditional gender roles and hierarchies. In the book, he discusses the differences between men and women and the biological basis for certain behaviors and traits. Some readers have accused him of promoting outdated and sexist ideas about gender, arguing that his perspective is regressive and harmful. Another controversial aspect of "12 Rules for Life" is Peterson's emphasis on personal responsibility and individualism. He argues that individuals should take responsibility for their own lives and strive to improve themselves, rather than blaming society or external forces for their problems. While this message resonates with many readers, others have criticized it for lacking empathy and understanding of systemic issues that can impact an individual's life circumstances. Peterson's conservative political views have also been a source of controversy. He has been criticized for his opposition to political correctness and his support for traditional values and institutions. Some readers have accused him of promoting a conservative agenda and aligning himself with far-right ideologies. Despite the controversy surrounding "12 Rules for Life," the book has resonated with many readers and has become a bestseller.
