The song was reportedly one of the last tracks written and recorded for the album, coming together relatively quickly towards the end of the sessions. The song's iconic piano riff was conceived by frontman Chris Martin . He has mentioned that the riff came to him late one night in Liverpool and was inspired by the English rock band Muse. Initially, the band was unsure if "Clocks" would fit the overall tone of "A Rush of Blood to the Head," which was already nearing completion. However, after their manager Phil Harvey urged them to work on it, the song quickly took shape. All four members of Coldplay (Guy Berryman, Jonny Buckland, Will Champion, and Chris Martin) are credited as songwriters. The track was co-produced by the band and British record producer Ken Nelson, who also produced their debut album "Parachutes." "Clocks" was released as the third single from the album in the UK in March 2003 and in the US in May 2003. üèÜ Chart Performance, Awards, and Legacy #9 UK Singles Chart #29 US Billboard Hot 100 Grammy Record of the Year (2004) Signature Coldplay Song "Clocks" achieved significant commercial success and critical acclaim, becoming one of Coldplay's most recognizable and enduring songs.
