Infobox references Chemical compound Isopropyl aminoethylmethyl phosphonite (NATO designation QL ), also known as O -(2-diisopropylaminoethyl) O ′ -ethyl methylphosphonite , is a precursor chemical to the nerve agent VX and VR-56. It is a colorless liquid with a strong fishy odor, and is slightly soluble in water. [ 1 ] Synthesis [ edit ] QL is manufactured by the transesterification of diethyl methylphosphonite with 2-(diisopropylamino)ethanol . [ 2 ] [ 3 ] Uses in chemical warfare [ edit ] QL is a component in binary chemical weapons , mainly VX nerve agent . [ 4 ] It, along with methylphosphonyl difluoride (DF), was developed during the 1980s in order to replace an aging stockpile of unitary chemical weapons . [ 4 ] QL is listed as a Schedule 1 chemical by the Chemical Weapons Convention . [ 5 ] Toxicity [ edit ] QL itself is a relatively non-toxic chemical. [ 6 ] However, when reacting with sulfur, the corresponding sulfide of QL isomerizes into the highly toxic VX molecule. [ 4 ] [ 6 ] References [ edit ] ^ "Isopropyl aminoethylmethyl phosphonite" .
