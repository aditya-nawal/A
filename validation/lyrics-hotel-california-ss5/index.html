Writer(s): Glenn Lewis Frey, Don Felder, Donald Hugh Henley "Hotel California" is the title track from the album and it was released as a single in February 1977. Eagles decided to make this song a single though its length and format were completely unlike the radio-friendly singles those days: the song is more than six minutes long, it's hard to dance to it, it has a long introduction and a long guitar solo. "It's our interpretation of the high life in Los Angeles. It's basically a song about the dark underbelly of the American dream and about excess in America, which is something we knew a lot about." - Don Henley. The word "colitas" means "little buds" or "little tails" in Spanish. The song reached number one position on the Billboard Hot 100 and won the Grammy Award for Record of the Year (1977).
