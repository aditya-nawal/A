History of Creation "Stupid Love" was officially released on February 28, 2020, as the lead single from Lady Gaga's sixth studio album, Chromatica . However, the song's journey to release was marked by a significant leak in January 2020, which led to the track going viral on social media. Gaga humorously responded to the leak on Twitter with the message "can y'all stop." The song was written by Lady Gaga, Max Martin, Ely Rise, and its producers, BloodPop and Tchami. This marked Gaga's first collaboration with the acclaimed hitmaker Max Martin. According to BloodPop, "Stupid Love" was the song that initiated his work with Gaga on the Chromatica album. The creation process involved BloodPop making a track, Gaga singing over it, and then sending it to Martin, who refined parts before Gaga wrote the final lyrics. Gaga has stated that the album, and by extension this song, represents her journey to healing through music and dance, putting her heart and pain into energetically pure and fun music. Despite the leak, Gaga and her team decided to stick with "Stupid Love" as the lead single, as they had invested months in developing the music video and choreography, believing the complete artistic piece was more than just the audio. Cultural Influence "Stupid Love" marked a significant cultural moment as Lady Gaga's return to her signature dance-pop sound, a move celebrated by critics and fans alike as a "return to form." Upon its release, the song debuted at number five on the Billboard Hot 100, making Gaga the fifth artist in history to have a top 10 hit in the 2000s, 2010s, and 2020s. It also reached the top 10 in numerous other countries, including a #5 debut in the UK. The accompanying music video, famously shot entirely on an iPhone 11 Pro, received widespread attention for its futuristic, cyberpunk aesthetic and its narrative of warring tribes on the planet Chromatica. The video introduced the visual themes of the Chromatica era, including the "Kindness Punks" and the sine wave symbol, which became central to the album's identity. The song was performed memorably at the 2020 MTV Video Music Awards and was a staple on The Chromatica Ball stadium tour. While not her biggest chart-topper, "Stupid Love" successfully re-established Gaga as a dominant force in pop music and set the stage for the critically and commercially successful Chromatica album.
