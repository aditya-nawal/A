King This song was written as an answer to the songs "Southern Man" and "Alabama" by Neil Young , who wrote them about the racism and slavery in the South. This track is kind of an anthem for the state of Alabama and it is often played during sport events there. Neil Young later stated in his auto-biography, "Waging Heavy Peace", "My own song 'Alabama' richly deserved the shot Lynyrd Skynyrd gave me with their great record. I don't like my words when I listen to it. They are accusatory and condescending, not fully thought out, and too easy to misconstrue." These lyrics contain a reference to the studio musicians at Muscle Shoals Sound Studios, where Lynyrd Skynyrd recorded in 1970-1972. Neil Young performed this at a memorial to the three members of Lynyrd Skynyrd which died in a plane crash in 1977.
