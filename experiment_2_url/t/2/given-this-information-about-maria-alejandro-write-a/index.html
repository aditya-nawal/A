Racism is an ugly, unfortunate reality in society. Despite popular belief, racism isn‚Äôt just a matter of yelling slurs and posting offensive social media comments‚Äîit‚Äôs a prejudiced type of thinking that hurts many people on a daily basis. If you‚Äôre a victim of racism, focus on preserving your mental health, which may make it easier to cope with the toxic language and behavior. Try not to view your statement as a confrontation‚Äîinstead, focus on starting a new conversation with the person in question. There‚Äôs also ‚Äúinstitutional racism‚Äù and ‚Äústructural racism,‚Äù which are perpetuated through organizations and governments, respectively. 2 Be on the lookout for racist behavior. Racism can rear its ugly head in a lot of different ways, and isn‚Äôt just limited to cruel slurs and mean remarks. [12] X Trustworthy Source American Psychological Association Leading scientific and professional organization of licensed psychologists Go to source Racial microaggressions are subtle types of racism that show up in conversation, or in a person‚Äôs everyday behavior.
