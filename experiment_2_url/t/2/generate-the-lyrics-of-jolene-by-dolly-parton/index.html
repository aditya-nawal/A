The song became one of Parton's most iconic and frequently covered tracks. AllMusic Dolly Parton has shared the real-life inspirations behind the song. The name "Jolene" came from a young red-headed fan who asked for her autograph after a show. Parton thought her name was beautiful and decided to use it. The emotional core of the song, however, was inspired by a red-headed bank teller who Parton believed was flirting with her husband, Carl Dean, shortly after they were married. Parton recalled, "She got this terrible crush on my husband. And he just loved going to the bank because she paid him so much attention. It was like a running joke between us... I wrote the song 'Jolene' based on that." NPR The song was recorded at RCA Studio B in Nashville, Tennessee. Its simple yet powerful arrangement and Parton's heartfelt vocal delivery quickly resonated with audiences. "Jolene" showcased Parton's exceptional songwriting talent, her ability to craft relatable narratives with deep emotional honesty, and her distinctive vocal style. It became a cornerstone of her career and a classic in the country music genre.
