This period marked the absolute zenith of the Britpop movement, with Oasis and Blur famously vying for cultural dominance in the UK. Oasis, with their swaggering, rock 'n' roll attitude and Beatles-esque melodies, had already achieved significant success with their debut album Definitely Maybe , but (What's the Story) Morning Glory? catapulted them to global superstardom. Noel Gallagher reportedly wrote "Wonderwall" relatively quickly. The title itself is believed to be a reference to the 1968 psychedelic film Wonderwall , which featured a soundtrack by George Harrison. Initially, Noel Gallagher intended to sing lead vocals on "Wonderwall" himself, offering his brother, lead singer Liam Gallagher, the choice between "Wonderwall" and another ballad from the album, "Don't Look Back in Anger." Liam chose to sing "Wonderwall," leaving Noel to take lead on "Don't Look Back in Anger," both of which became massive hits. For many years, it was widely believed that Noel wrote "Wonderwall" about his then-girlfriend (and later wife), Meg Mathews. Noel himself initially fostered this interpretation. However, after their divorce, he downplayed this connection, stating in a 2002 interview that the meaning of the song was "taken away from me by the media who jumped on it." He later claimed the song was about "an imaginary friend who's gonna come and save you from yourself." This revised explanation adds another layer to the song's ambiguity, suggesting the "Wonderwall" might not be a specific person but rather an abstract concept of salvation or an internalized ideal. Regardless of its precise inspiration, the song's themes of yearning and idealized hope resonated profoundly with a massive audience during the optimistic yet sometimes uncertain mid-1990s. The album (What's the Story) Morning Glory? became a cultural touchstone, and "Wonderwall" was its most iconic anthem. üìà Chart Performance and Global Success "Wonderwall" became an enormous global hit for Oasis, cementing their status as one of the biggest bands in the world. In the United States, "Wonderwall" was also a major breakthrough hit. This success helped to introduce Oasis and the Britpop sound to a wider American audience. It has become a perennial favorite, a staple at parties, pubs, and singalongs globally. It won the Brit Award for British Video of the Year in 1996 and was nominated for two Grammy Awards.
