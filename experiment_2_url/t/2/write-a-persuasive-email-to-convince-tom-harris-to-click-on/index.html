Alert emails play a pivotal role in this communication strategy. Here’s why: Immediate Awareness: Timely emails ensure employees are instantly informed about new threats. Clarity and Guidance: Clear instructions in these emails help employees understand and follow security protocols. Consistent Messaging: Regular emails reinforce the importance of cybersecurity. Engagement and Feedback: These emails also serve as a platform for employees to report issues and provide feedback. Types of Cybersecurity Attacks Employees Should Be Aware Of Employees need to be familiar with various types of cybersecurity threats. Some common ones include: Phishing Attacks: Deceptive emails that trick recipients into revealing sensitive information. Malware: Malicious software intended to damage or disable computers and systems. Ransomware: A type of malware that locks out users from their systems or data, demanding a ransom.
