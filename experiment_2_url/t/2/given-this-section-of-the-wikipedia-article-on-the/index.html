Download Cite Comment Abstract This working paper reconstructs the unfolding of events in India in March-April 1919 that led to the Jallianwala Bagh massacre. It evaluates the events and administrative and military response from a legal history perspective. It also argues that Jallianwala Bagh massacre was not a unique event in itself but was the culmination of colonial rage against a broader anti-colonial mobilisation.
