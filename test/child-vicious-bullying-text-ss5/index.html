Introduction Child abuse is a widespread and alarming issue of Pakistan because it has been left largely uncontrolled due many reasons. The effects of verbal abuse are quite negative and devastating on child psychology. Without any doubt, all the stakeholders have witnessed many instances of verbal abuse in schools. Mostly reported, by parents that the teachers in the schools used to shout on their children, calling them by different names and threatened them that their parents will be informed about their misbehavior. According to the recent reports, the ratio of child abuse is not only high but largely practiced phenomenon in Pakistan. However, the problem still exists in the society and consequences not are according to the real impression. Child abuse is prevalent in all the educational institutions. The concern authorities fail to control this act and put complete ban on this practice. The problems related to verbal abuse were not adequately highlighted at least in the research carried out in Pakistan. It was, therefore, considered imperative to warrant a proper study and to evaluate what is the impact of verbal abuse on children psychology in Peshawar city, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province of Pakistan. Verbal Abuse Definitions of verbal abuse vary from one author to another. It usually include threats, offensive language, comments with harsh words, uses of swear words, yelling, shouting, teasing, ridiculing, passing nasty remarks, taunting to make the victim feel that they are not worthy of love or respect. It very well fits into the category of emotional torture that has long lasting repercussions. Review Literature Wolf (Oct 11, 2013) concluded that verbal abuse is a specific type of emotional abuse. He further added, that the most commonly form of verbal abuse is insulting, threatening, demeaning or derogatory language, shaming, intimidating. Verbal abuse may lead to stress, depression, physical disorders, and other damage. Gadit (2011) cited that verbal abuse now becomes a common phenomenon around the world. Mostly it is believed, by the people that verbal abuse brings less harm to students and has no devastating impact on child psychology. This is totally a wrong notion, now it has been universally accepted fact that verbal abuse does brings disastrous consequences. Jabeen (2014) viewed that among South Asian Countries, Sri Lanka was the first country to formally recognize child abuse and protection issues. A study was conducted by American Academy of Pediatrics (2005), revealed that 15 % 15 \% 15% of the children become victim of verbal abuse by the teachers. The students who were well- behaved and welladjusted having low risk of becoming victim of verbal aggression by the students. Casarjian(2000) conducted a study including 700 North American students. The main emphasis of the study was to examine whether students were subjected to verbal abuse or not? It was found that approximately one quarter of students reported that they become target of verbal abuse by their teachers especially boys as compared to girls. Pace (2013) viewed that verbal abuse acts as battering on psychology. It does not show any visible scars but the pain of verbal abuse is deep long-lasting and recovery can be very challenging. It usually includes lying, taunting, withholding, disguised as jocks and yelling. Sharpe (2011) investigated the teacher abuse in Ontario’s elementary schools. The study revealed that teachers have, and continue to use, abusive behaviors against students. There is a need to implement specific rules and actions within the school and to intervene at the teacher level to reduce abuse by teachers. Stress was recognized one of the major causes for abusing students in the class room and the impact of such behaviors is widespread. The study further revealed that approximately 2 % 2 \% 2% to 11 % 11 \% 11% of teachers engage in abusive behaviours against students. There are alarming figures. According to the American Academy of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry bullying Statistics (2013) verbal bullying is practiced by girls. They mostly used it to show their superiority and dominancy. However, boys also use this technique in order to avoid the trouble that can come with physically bullying someone else. Sadruddin (2011) concluded that the issues of children were not given any importance in Pakistan during the last few years. Their rights were totally ignored and they are treated as a minor creature. Everyone knows about the negative impact of verbal abuse but still this problem is not well addressed. The most serious impact is of psychological nature. Impact of Child Abuse on Child Psychology The impact of psychological abuse in schools is destructive. The victim of a verbally abusive person slowly loses herself. The child becomes hollow before we realize how awful life become. Verbally abused child spoke fever sentences and used fever words. They did not enjoy inventing stories. The storytelling of abused children showed a lack of essential curiosity. It was observed through different studies that those children who receive negative experiences in school become victim of mental stress, depression, emotional instability, and do not want to attend the school any more. The impact of verbal abuse on child is very dangerous. The children who become suffer from verbal abuse fall into category of depression, lack of confidence, mental stress, do not like to have social contacts, discontinue their studies, and feeling degrade. Conclusion Violence against children in Pakistan is strongly entrenched in the Pakistani society. Poor attention has been given to solve the sensitive issues related to child rights. Violations of child rights are clearly observed in Pakistan but no one try to deal these issues with high concern and commitment. Children need secure and conducive environment. Their voices must be heard and trusted and every possible step must be taken to provide them healthy and safe atmosphere. In Pakistan, verbal abuse is rampant and visible. Many children reported that they become victim of verbal abuse on daily basis by the teachers, parents and friends .The children who are constant exposed to verbal abuse develop hatred against teacher, school and that very subject. Some of them drop-out among those who remain in school, a large number of them, become very shy and stop taking part in the classroom activities. Since then confidence is shuttered. They do not try to take initiatives in the class and remain pre-occupied by the thought how to avoid punishment. Such a class has a threatening environment that kills the fun part of learning. We do not have exact data on this particular issue but consequences are very obvious and speak a lot about it. In Pakistan, there are no legal laws, especially to deal with the problem of verbal abuse, or to suggest punishment to teachers who practiced this act in the classroom. Cultural approval promotes and sustains attitudes that overlook or support the use of physical or mental violence against children where such approval is weak or losing ground, the ineffectiveness of laws and implementation regimes fails to protect children from any kind of abuse. Furthermore, legislative and administrative initiatives to address abuse of children did not surface in 2012. The government even failed to pass a series of child protection laws at provincial level. The issue of verbal abuse against children cannot be treated solely through policy and administration. People and communities need to be apprised of the harmful effects of psychological violence on the well being of the child. Community support and approval can go a long way in ensuring the effectiveness of policy, legislative and administrative initiatives at the grassroots. There is a need for further research in the area of school-based violence. Specifically, there is a need for research, which explores the experiences of teachers. In so doing, this will provide teachers with support, which will hopefully counteract violent attacks and minimize the negative effects of being exposed to violence at school.
